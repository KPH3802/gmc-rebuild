## Authorized Composer Contract

The P6-03 composer is authorized as an inert, deterministic pure-function surface with the following contract:

- It consumes a P6-01 `SignalIntent` by value.
- It consumes a P6-02 `EligibilityDecision` by value.
- It consumes the merged P4 safety verdict surface by value, preferably as an already-rendered `SafetyVerdict`. If the implementation uses `RuntimeShell.evaluate()` directly, it must do so only as a read-only evaluation of already-constructed in-memory protocol fakes supplied by the caller, with no instantiation of runtime infrastructure inside the composer.
- It returns a single frozen, slotted decision dataclass, provisionally `PositionDecision`, describing whether the system would trade or would skip.
- It defines a closed two-member outcome enum, provisionally `PositionDecisionOutcome` with `WOULD_TRADE` and `WOULD_SKIP`.
- It defines a closed reason enum, provisionally `PositionDecisionReason`, covering:
  - `ELIGIBILITY_INELIGIBLE`
  - `SAFETY_HEARTBEAT_STALE`
  - `SAFETY_KILL_SWITCH_NOT_ARMED`
  - `SAFETY_RECONCILIATION_NOT_CLEAN`
  - `SAFETY_VERDICT_NOT_CLEAR`
- It enforces the biconditional that a trade outcome has no reasons and a skip outcome has at least one reason.
- It reports all applicable skip reasons in canonical declaration order.
- It preserves upstream closed shapes; it does not expand P6-01, P6-02, P3, P4, or P5 data models.
- It is deterministic, side-effect-free, mutation-free, hidden-state-free, network-free, filesystem-free, scheduler-free, broker-free, and runtime-inert.
