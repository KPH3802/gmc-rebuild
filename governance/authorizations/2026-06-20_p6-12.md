Date: 2026-06-20
Authorizer: Kevin

existing dry-run operator entry point as a new opt-in flag
`--show-reconciliation`, alongside (and independent of) the existing
`--emit-json` and `--emit-reconciliation-json` flags. The flag appends a

## Closed Inputs / Outputs

- **New flag:** `--show-reconciliation` (boolean, no argument). Supported
  **only** with `--source insider_cluster` (rejected with a non-zero
  argparse error on the synthetic source, mirroring `--emit-json` and
  `--emit-reconciliation-json`). When present, a deterministic text block
  is appended to stdout after the existing human summary. The block is
  independent of `--emit-json` and `--emit-reconciliation-json`; any
  subset of the three flags may be passed in one run.
- **Reconciliation performed:** the same pure, deterministic, read-only
  **self-comparison** as P6-11 — `reconcile_dry_run_positions(
  simulated=portfolio,
  expected=ExpectedPositions.from_simulated_portfolio(portfolio),
  reconciliation_status=report.reconciliation_status)`. By construction
  this yields `outcome=MATCH` with every simulated position in `matches`
  and empty difference lists. The `reconciliation_status` is echoed
  verbatim from the cycle's `DailyReport`. No real account, no broker, no
  external expected-positions source.
- **Output shape:** a deterministic plain-text block headed
  `dry_run_reconciliation:` with the outcome, reconciliation status,
  summary counts, and the four canonical lists (matches,
  quantity_mismatches, only_in_simulated, only_in_expected) — `(none)` for
  each empty list. No emojis, no colors, no clock read, no timestamp.
